2. Eligibility

You can get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) if you have an illness or disability which affects your ability to work and you:

  • are under state pension age - check your state pension age
  • are not getting Jobseekers Allowance
  • are unemployed
  • are self-employed
  • work for an employer but cannot get Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)
  • were getting SSP which has now stopped
  • are a student who is getting Disability Living Allowance or Personal Independence Payment
  • have a valid fit note from your doctor for any period of illness after the first 7 days

Even if you can't get paid ESA, it is worthwhile claiming to get National Insurance credits that count towards your State Pension.

Terminal illness

If you have a terminal illness and less than six months to live, you still have to claim but will get a decision faster.
